Not demos. Not decks. Actual things that run, that work, that people can use today.

I got here by building. The fastest way I know to understand something is to make it real.

What I Do

I design workflows, build tools, and shape product experiences by building alongside AI. My role is part implementation, part product, and part experimentation.

I work best in the space between a good idea and a working product. That gap is usually where the real problems live.

AI moves fast. The people using it still need clarity, trust, and something that actually fits their day. That is what I focus on.

Questions You Might Have

What does “bridges between AI and people” mean?

It means making AI usable. I connect technical capability with human behavior, product decisions, and real workflows so the system actually helps someone.

Are you technical or product-facing?

Both. That is the point. I can work close to the system, but I care just as much about the person on the other side of it.

What kinds of things do you build?

Tools, workflows, automations, prototypes, and product experiences that help people interact with AI more naturally and effectively.

Why does this matter?

Because powerful systems fail when people cannot trust them, use them, or understand them. The bridge is what makes the technology matter.

What are you focused on now?

Building practical AI tools, shaping better interfaces for AI systems, and closing the gap between what the technology can do and what people actually need.
